Creatine

Home Page

Creatine is one of the most widely researched and commonly used supplements in strength and resistance training. Studies have shown that creatine supplementation increases the amount of phosphocreatine stored in muscles, which helps the body produce energy more efficently during short, high-intensity activities such as weightlifting and sprinting. As a result, individuals who supplement with creatine are often able to preform more repetitions, lift heavier weights, or sustain higher training intensity compared to those who do not use creatine.

Facts About Creatine

  • Creatine helps improve strength and preformance by increasing the body's ability to produce energy
  • Creatine suppoters muscle growth indirectly by allowing users to train harder and preform more repetitions
  • Creatine is considered a safe and healthy supplement as it is one of the most well-researched suppliments in sports nutrition

While creatine does not directly build muscle, creatine enhances the body's ability to train at higher intensities, which can lead to greater long-term gains when combined with proper exercise and nutrition. Many common misconceptions about creatine have been disproven through scientific research, showing it to be safe when used correctly. Understanding how creatine works allows adults and athletes alike to make informed decisons about supplementation. When creatine is used with constant training and responsible nutrition habits, it can be a valuable tool for supporting fitness goals.
